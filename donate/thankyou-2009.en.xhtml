Thank you for your support!

FSFE would like to thank everyone who supported our work in 2009. Your contributions as volunteer, Fellow or sponsor make FSFE's work possible. Distributed support secures FSFE's independence, which is why every small contribution is important!

But we also receive large contributions. Last year's top donors were the LinuxHotel, Black Duck Software, Intevation GmbH, freiheit.com technologies GmbH, and Tarent GmbH. Our sincere thanks and appreciation go to all of them, but two deserve a special mention.

We're looking forward to cooking with Reinhard Wiesemann at his LinuxHotel. As our visionary patron, he has supported FSFE every single month since it was created and he also was the largest contributor over the past year. A special mention also goes to Intevation GmbH, which has not only donated at patron level, but has also sponsored working time and expenses for FSFE co-founder Bernhard Reiter, who over the years has put much time, effort and knowledge into the organisation.

The past year was both challenging and successful for FSFE. We have succeeded in something that no Free Software Foundation has attempted so far: A change in the organisation's leadership, with a new executive team. The former president Georg Greve did splendid work in taking FSFE from its founding in 2001 to the internationally respected organisation that it is today, working very hard alongside many dedicated people. FSFE is designed to be bigger than any one person, and it's good to see that this design principle is working out in practice.

In 2009, we battled Microsoft's monopoly in the browser market, pushed hard for Open Standards in the European public sector, and helped to launch a legal review on Free Software. At the UN level, we worked to have Open Standards and Free Software included [PDF] in the global activities of the World Intellectual Property Organisation (WIPO). We asked candidates for the German federal elections about their positions on Free Software, and helped the Fellowship to launch pdfreaders.org as a campaign for free PDF readers. We were also happy to welcome a new FSFE country team in Finland. All this work has helped to make 2009 a good year for Free Software.

The year 2010 brings many new challenges for Free Software, and a number of old ones still stand. In Brussels, we will push for competition, Open Standards and interoperability, and continue to fight proprietary monopolies. At the UN level, we will continue to drive the slow reorientation of the World Intellectual Property Organisation towards Open Standards and Free Software. We will make cooperation between FSFE's country teams more effective, and the relaunched Edu-Team will find ways to get more Free Software into schools and universities.

We have successfully built the Freedom Task Force (FTF) into Europe's foremost centre of Free Software legal and licensing knowledge. In 2010, we will use this knowledge to help both the community and industry to build governance models around Free Software. We are also making the FTF's knowledge available through our professional services.

All these things are only possible because of people like you who are supporting FSFE by sharing their time, their resources, and their ideas. If there is an issue that you think FSFE should deal with, please don't hesitate to get in touch. I invite you to work with us this year, and hope that we can count on your support also in 2010. Together, we can achieve great things for Free Software.

Once again, thank you.

Best regards,
Karsten Gerloff
President
