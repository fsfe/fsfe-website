#ilovefs Report 2015

On Saturday, the 14 February, people all over the world showed Free Software contributors their appreciation. It was the fifth year the Free Software Foundation Europe (FSFE) asked people to participate at the “I Love Free Software”-Day. This report shows a variety of different love declarations that happened this day in blog posts, pictures, comics, a poem, and a #ilovefs Android library. The FSFE thanks everybody who motivated Free Software contributors. For everybody who was not able to participate this year, mark 14 February 2015 in your calendar for next year's “I love Free Software”-Day.

Will you be my cryptovalentine?

This year many people focused their activities on -- what FSFE's president Karsten Gerloff described as -- “powerful tools that help us defend and regain the freedoms we’ve lost, and the ones we’ve given up”: as Our sister organisation encouraged people to ask someone they like -- romantically or otherwise -- to be their “cryptovalentine”. Besides like André Ockers, FSFE's vice president Matthias Kirschner, and many others they expressed their appreciation to all the hardworking Free Software contributors who work for our privacy.
