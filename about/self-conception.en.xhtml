Europe, August 2004

Free Software Foundation Europe

Self-Conception

Declaration

    We, the people of the Free Software Foundation Europe (FSFE), see ourselves as Europeans from different cultures with the shared goal of cooperation across cultures and developing a common culture of cooperation from regional to global level.

    Together, we form a non-profit non-governmental organization and network that itself is part of a global network of equals with common goals and visions. We are not representative for anyone but ourselves and our work. Our common work and dedication to freedom in all aspects of the digital society is what defines us.

    With a Free Software community that is growing faster every day, it becomes more important to keep the vision of Free Software healthy, solid and alive. In some cases, increased political interest in our issues also creates desire to instrument them for personal particular interests regardless of the medium- and long-term effects.

    Therefore, central component of our work is keeping the legal, political and social base of Free Software strong, secure and free of particular interests. This requires deep understanding of Free Software and related issues. More importantly, it requires above all being primarily committed to the long-term vision.

    We understand this sometimes means having to accept short-term or even medium-term disadvantages in order to remain true to our principles, which will generally be hard to communicate and occasionally even unpopular.


Principles

    We feel strongly committed to the principles of democracy, transparency, plurality, consistency, reliability and focus.

    In the light of high responsibility in legal issues and the need to remain decisive even in difficult times, our structure aims to implement the above principles as well as possible.


Structural Overview

    Voluntary work and contribution is the fundament on which everything rests. Different levels of involvement exist. The personal level of involvement is primarily a personal decision.

    Everyone is welcome to occasionally, regularly or permanently participate in the activities of the Free Software Foundation Europe (FSFE) and make its activities their own.

    If you wish to become part of the Free Software Foundation Europe, you can join one of our teams -- either geographically or thematically -- and take responsibility to work and act in the name of the FSFE.

    The country-based teams are generally coordinated by the members of the association (see below) responsible for these countries. The thematic teams are either coordinated by members of the association, members of the teams or members of an associated organization (see below).

    If you wish to become part of the Free Software Foundation Europe but do not have the time to do work, you might also choose to become a fellow of the FSFE.

    If your association pursues similar goals to those of the Free Software Foundation Europe and wishes to establish formal cooperation, it can become an associate organization of the FSFE, thus becoming part of the FSFE network.

    The skeleton of the Free Software Foundation Europe is the formal and legally established association with its chapters in the different countries. Membership in this association requires the highest commitment, measured in years of dedication and the responsibility to put long-term consensus over personal opinion.

    This organization is fundamentally democratic and all parts of the FSFE -- members of the association, members of the teams, as well as fellows and members of associate organizations -- are welcome to actively participate in its decision finding processes.

    Members of the association are usually proposed by members of the teams and the associate organizations from their countries. They are then approved by the general assembly of the FSFE association.

    Although voluntary contributions in terms of work and resources are the grounds of our work, we recognize that some of these tasks cannot be undertaken as part-time activities and therefore require full-time activity.

    Normal employees of the Free Software Foundation Europe are part of the executive only and do not become part of the general decision finding processes.

    Any decision about employment/payment must ultimately be agreed upon by the general assembly of the members of the association.

    Main criterion for this decision are the needs of the Free Software Foundation Europe in terms of successfully pursuing its goals. Whenver possible, we try to hire the person for the job that has already done the job without getting paid -- either within the FSFE structures or without -- because knowledge, dedication and initiative are virtues embedded in the FSFE.

    Ideally we will allow someone to dedicate themselves fully to a task that was already important enough to them to receive much of their available resources.


Decision Processes

    We, the people of the Free Software Foundation Europe believe in consensus. By default, we always seek to base our work on the consensus -- and sometimes compromise -- of our active people.

    Also we believe in capability for quick and decisive action, when necessary. For this reason we have established the FSFE association and its extended executive committees on European and national levels. These provide fall-back structures and procedures that are determined, monitored and controlled through democratic processes.

    This approach was adopted in search of a structure that will allow transparency, plurality and participation, at the same time remaining as lightweight as possible.

    While ensuring the possibility of participation by all parts of the Free Software Foundation Europe -- members of the association, members of the teams and members of associate organizations -- it allows the FSFE to act quickly when necessary and maintain a strong organizational long-term consistency.
