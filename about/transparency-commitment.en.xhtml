Transparency Commitment

With the initiative “transparent civil society”, Transparency International Germany provides a guide how to make a transparency commitment. This page guides you to the relevant information on our website.

  1. Name, place, address, founding year.
  2. Full constitution, information about concrete goals and how we want to reach them.
  3. Date and other details of the last writing from the finance authorities, that we are tax deductible (in Germany).
  4. Name and function of our decision makers.
  5. Reports about the activities of the organisation are available as general news, monthly newsletters, the annual minutes from the general assembly, on our mailinglists, etc.
  6. How much staff we have and how much they work, is also available on our team page.
  7. Information on how we use our money.
  8. Legal connections with other organisations: We have sister organisations around the world, but are not legally connected with them.
  9. We list the names of legal bodies which donated more than 10% of our yearly budget on our donor page. People who donate more than 10% of our yearly budget are only listed if they agree, else we list them there as “anonymous donation from private persons”.
