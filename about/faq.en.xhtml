Free Software Foundation Europe

Frequently Answered Questions (FAQ)

  • Q: Why don't you have an "associate membership" like the FSF North America?

    A: The FSF Europe has considered this in the past - in fact we have been receiving requests for something like this as early as 2001. We did consider it, but have decided against it for the FSF Europe.

    Our main reason for deciding against it was that we felt it was problematic to call normal donors "members" if they don't get the right to vote. This is not so unusual in the United States, especially for U.S. book clubs and such, but for a European organization working in the political field it didn't seem fitting.

    We are thinking about other possible alternatives to give people some goodies for supporting the FSF Europe, though.

    The sponsoring buttons were a first step, but there will be more.

  • Q: Who are the members of the FSF Europe?

    A: People from different European countries, usually with a long history in Free Software. A list of members is available here.

  • Q: Someone claimed to be working for you, is this true?

    A: We have many people who work with and for us on a regular basis, so even if you get contacted by someone with an email address that does not seem related, this person may be working in our name. Chances are you will find that person on our list of Permanent Volunteers. Otherwise please ask us if you want to be sure.

  • Q: What is your legal status/structure?

    A: As Europe is still quite a ways from being legally united, a modular solution had to be found. You can find more information here.

    A: They are online here.
