Towards a
"World Intellectual Wealth Organisation"

Supporting the Geneva Declaration

    The Geneva Declaration is an impressive step towards the creation of a broad coalition of people and organisations demanding that the international community re-think the goals and mechanisms for awarding monopoly control over different kinds of knowledge. It is full with constructive, concrete suggestions on which aspects ought to be tackled first, and provides ample and insightful argumentation as to why more carefully balanced copyright and patent bargains are crucial for all of humankind at this time.

    We are convinced that new questions sometimes require new answers, not more careful repetition of old answers. A World Intellectual Property Organisation will always, understandably, lean towards applying the pre-selected tool-set of monopolisation currently referred to as Intellectual Property; a term that we feel being ideologically charged and dangerously unaware of the significant differences that exist between the many areas of law it is supposed to refer to.

    While it may look at better, possibly more socially sustainable ways of granting ownership-like monopolies over different forms of knowledge, WIPO will not have an easy time looking for alternative solutions. WIPO is not what we need.

    We need a World Intellectual Wealth Organisation, dedicated to the research and promotion of novel and imaginative ways to encourage the production and dissemination of knowledge. Granting limited monopolies and limited control over some kinds of knowledge will most likely be part of this new organisations tool-set, but not the only one, and maybe not even the most important one.

    We endorse and support the Geneva Declaration, and invite its drafters, signatories, and the United Nations to start thinking now not only about what the role of WIPO should be, but rather what kind of organisation we need in its place.
