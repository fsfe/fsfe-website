mEUcrosoft contracts

The FSFE has recently expressed its concern for the lock-in into Microsoft software and licenses that the EU experiences since a number of years. To understand the issue, it is helpful to develop a baisc understanding of how the EU awards its IT contracts.

As the EU is a public institution financed by the people, it is required to allocate its resources in a fair and transparent way. In terms of IT contracts, this means that the EU is supposed to consider alternating solutions in a fair and competitive way in order to arrive at the best solution possible.

The process described above carries the name "Open Call for Tender". It essentially means that the EU announces its requirements for a specific IT solutions, and providers of said solutions apply and compete in order to be chosen. This, of course, includes providers of Free Software.

Despite the abovementioned procedure, the EU has repeatedly entered contractual agreements with Microsoft through a process called 'negotiated procedure'. What this means is that a deal is made without the required level of competitiveness, with questionable arguments.
