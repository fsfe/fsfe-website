In Europe, the CONVENTION ON THE GRANT OF EUROPEAN PATENTS (EUROPEAN PATENT CONVENTION) (published in 1973 and revised December, 17th 1991) explicitly excludes from the field of patentable inventions programs for computers in the In the article 52.2.

Despite of this, the European Patent Office granted a large number of patents on programs, some of which are listed in the European Software Patents: Assorted Examples. Each of them can be checked at their original location or by substituting patent by the appropriate patent number in the following URL: 

	http://l2.espacenet.com/dips/viewer?PN=EP0487110&CY=ep&LG=en&DB=EPD

In June 1997, the european officials officialy decided to reconsider the patentability of programs in a Green Paper named Promoting innovation through patents. In November 2000 a Diplomatic Conference produced an ACT REVISING THE CONVENTION ON THE GRANT OF EUROPEAN PATENTS in which computer programs are confirmed to be out of the scope of patentable inventions.
