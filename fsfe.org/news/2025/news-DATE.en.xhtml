“They're not alone in their fight for software freedom“

Every year on 14. of February we celebrate “I Love Free Software Day”, thanking all who contribute to Free Software. Without all of you we wouldn’t have such a great ecosystem today! Our Polish group joined our celebrations on 2025 for the first time and we wanted to know more about them. Check out what Damian, our Poland group coordinator told us.

Since 2018 the Polish country team of the FSFE, the FSFE Poland is around. For some time there was a dull in the activities of the groups – but now they are back! Currently there are three local groups, Warsaw, Poznań and Wrocław supporting software freedom in Poland. Together they get up to quite a lot and are actively organising events and campaigns. For a good impression of their activity you can of course join their Matrix room (PL only).

Together they also decided to celebrate the I Love Free Software Day 2025. Taking this as the perfect opportunity to restart FSFE Poland. They organised three events to which everyone interested in Free Software was invited. Representatively, every event was visited by 20-30 people, there was lots of fun, interesting conversations with a focus on lesser known software, and lots of pizza!

FSFE: Hello Damian, we are glad that you could take the time for this interview. Can you tell us how many local groups there are in Poland? Can everybody who is interested join them?

Damian, coordinator of FSFE Poland: The Polish volunteers happen to be mostly from around Warsaw, Poznań and Wrocław so I'd say there are three groups overall. Of course anyone can join, as these are not strict groups at all . If your goals align with ours and Free Software is important for you, we invite you to participate in our Matrix rooms, especially if you'd be willing to help with our projects and activities.

FSFE: What did you do at the I Love Free software Day 2025?

Damian: This was our first time; we plan to celebrate the event each year in a more coordinated manner! For 2025, we managed to make a unified template for all our local activities and helped each other organise the event and in three cities: Wrocław, Poznań and Warsaw. The result were meetings of about 20-30+ people in each location. We talked about Free Software with emphasis on on those that are hidden, lesser-known projects, but important for us for some specific reason. Here you can find the code of our website, published by Michał Korczak under the ISC license.

FSFE: Was there something you liked very much about the celebrations?

Damian: I feel like it helped us unify our "frontlines" a little bit. People got to acknowledge that FSFE supports their work and – hopefully – that they're not alone in their fight for software freedom. I liked that we made a common template for the event and that we translated pre-talks to polish - huge kudos to Wiktor Przybylski, Paweł Wiczyński, Kacper from Hackerspace Łódź and others for their excellent work.

FSFE: Why do you think I Love Free Software Day is important and why should everybody be celebrating this day?

Damian: Because it raises awareness in the community about the importance of free software. It also shows the importance of work done by volunteers who help with the development of free software. "I Love Free Software Day" is one of the few opportunities to meet in person, where free software is the leading conversation topic.

FSFE: What are the activities the local groups do and is there something happening where new people are welcome to join and support the effort?

Damian: ou could say that each FSFE group is loosely associated around a hackerspace, which provided place to host the events in the three distinct cities this year. People feeling associated with the Free Software movement just naturally gravitate towards these places. We are using matrix channels as the sole medium of communication and everyone interested in Free Software is encouraged to join. I'd say our main activities are promoting the Free Software movement by organizing Free Software events in Poland: "Sesja Linuksowa" ("Linux Session"), "Poznańska Impreza Wolnego Oprogramowania" ("Poznań Free Software Party"), "Jesień Linuxowa" ("Linux Autumn") etc.
The FSFE volunteers are, in varying proportions, organisers of these events and I think it's now impossible to find a Free Software event without one of us being there. We're timidly trying to get more seriously into translations and have started writing reports regarding Free Software that I hope will appear by the end of the year.

FSFE: As last year, in 2025, you celebrated "I Love Free Software Day" for first time, what motivated you to participate?

Damian: Actually, the first “I Love Free Software Day” event I organised was in 2017 at Poznań University of Technology. I was a student and Free Software student's club president so it felt pretty natural at that time. I've already helped organising "Poznańska Impreza Wolnego Oprogramowania" ("Poznań Free Software Party") and Linux Presentation Days before. In 2016 I saw some FSFE stickers on someone's laptop and the person suggested that we could order stickers off FSFE website for our conference next time. I wanted to be part of the celebration because I loved the idea the first time and the timing for the event is ideal for a Free Software event because nothing much happens during the winter time.

FSFE: As a coordinator of the Poland group, you managed the communication for all three local groups. How was this from your perspective as coordinator and are there some pitfalls you can share with us that other country coordinators should watch out for?

Damian: I think I had huge advantage already because I personally know people from the other cities. I've studied in Poznań and the organisers are ex-members of the Free Software student's club. Their friends and I met Michał from Wrocław during “"Jesień Linuxowa". Him being one of the main organisers behind “Sesja Linuxowa” made him an ideal candidate to organise a way smaller event like "I Love Free Software Day". In reality I didn't have to coordinate much as I had very experienced organisers at hand that I could rely on. If I were to give a piece of advice to people coordinating things on country level scale, it is that you need to find people with initiative and a sense of responsibility to cooperate with. In any kind of relationship, input from both sides is required. I don't think it would really work in other communities if I tried to force my way onto people that don't feel comfortable in such a role.

FSFE: Do you already have plans for celebrating “I Love Free Software Day 2026”?

Damian: There is a LAN party culture that we inherited from previous organisers of “Poznańska Impreza Wolnego Oprogramowania” that I really cherish. I think it would be nice if the "I Love Free Software Day" had some LAN-Party-like online events alongside (like these informal communities organise: https://onfoss.org and https://fossgralnia.pl). There we could promote and raise awareness about this kind of software. Besides, I don't know if the theme is already announced but I hope everyone's aiming to affect more people, listen to more talks and eat more pizza.

FSFE: That sounds like a great party! We cannot wait to see your event! Keep our community posted! Thank you for your time and your work for the Free Software community!
