Radio stations granted awards for using Open Standards

Berlin/Vienna March 24. The Free Software Foundation Europe awarded Deutschlandradio and Radio Orange with the Document Freedom Day Prize 2010 for using Open Standards and and promoting them in society. The German team together with the Förderverein für eine freie informationelle Infrastruktur (FFII) will present the DFD cake displaying "rOGG on!" in Berlin and Cologne to Deutschlandradio. The Austrian DFD cake goes to the Vienna-based station Radio Orange 94.0.

Deutschlandradio and Radio Orange provide live streams of their programme in OGG-Vorbis format. Open Standards like OGG-Vorbis enable data transfers between software of different vendors. Thus, they offer users the possibility to use and transmit data independent from particular software vendors thereby using different types of hardware or software.

The Xiph.org foundation designed this format as a patent-free alternative to MP3 for compressing audio-files. In contrast to proprietary formats, Open Standards can be easily be used with Free Software. This makes Open Standards indispensable to the media, administration, companies, organisations, and PC-users.

FSFE is pleased that both radio stations set standards for the freedom of their listeners by using Open Standards.

Document Freedom Day

Document Freedom Day (DFD) is a global day for document liberation and Open Standards. Since 2008, it is celebrated on the last Wednesday in march. Over the past years, more than 200 teams of volunteers in over 60 countries have been imparting the value of Open Document Formats and Open Standards to others.
