Donations to the FSF Europe

There are many ways for people to support the work and goals of the FSF Europe. The most direct one is to help us as a volunteer by writing free software or free documentation. We also need people to help us with other tasks like manning tradeshow booths and such. If you are interested, you can take a look at the "What needs to be done?" page.

Another way to help the FSF Europe is to donate money that we will use to cover our expenses and pay people to work on and for Free Software.

The FSF Europe is a Europe-wide organization with a hub in Germany and the goal to have chapters in every European country. Wherever possible, donations to the FSF Europe will be made tax-deductible by registering the FSF Europe as a charitable organization according to local laws.

Become a supporter of the FSF Europe

The Free Software Foundation Europe wants to acknowledge its supporters and contributors in a visible fashion. Therefore we would like to ask you to send mail to <office@fsfeurope.org> and let us know where to send the receipt and thank-you letter. Also we'd like to list you on our "Thank GNUs" page.

If you donate in a year:

  • EUR 100, you'll become a Supporter of the FSF Europe.
  • EUR 500, you'll become a Contributor of the FSF Europe.
  • EUR 1000, you'll become a Sustaining Contributor of the FSF Europe.
  • EUR 5000, you'll become a Patron of the FSF Europe.

How to donate

The FSF Europe is still growing and your country may not yet have a chapter of the FSF Europe. In this case you can donate directly to the hub (see information below) or contact our office on information how to donate in a convenient way.

If you live outside Europe, you should also consider donating to our sister organization in the United States, the Free Software Foundation, instead.

Approval by the authorities usually takes some time. So it may be possible that although there is a chapter in your country already, donations may not yet be tax-deductable. Whether you can donate trusting in the charitable status to be confirmed later or whether you'll have to wait depends on local laws.

Please understand that we cannot guarantee tax-deductability in those cases.
