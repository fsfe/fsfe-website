Legal Structure

Vision

The vision of the FSF Europe is largely influenced by the vision of a united Europe. We wanted to form one organisation in which we would work together as Europeans across cultural, language, social and country barriers.

This vision of Europe was (and is not) limited to the European Union, but encompasses all countries and people who feel part of the European culture and spirit.

By standing together we are able to not only help each other in critical situations, but also gain inspiration from discussion.

But the European Union exists and it already is governing many countries in areas vital to Free Software and freedom in a digital society.

Together we are able to address all these areas simultaneously with one voice.

Implementation

Unfortunately, there is no legal basis to implement this vision 1:1 into a legal structure, so we needed to find a way to make the vision come through despite these legal shortcomings.

The "legal hack" that we came up with is a modular structure that allows us to expand it step by step into every country that feels part of Europe whether it is part of the European Union or not.

The Hub

The Hub is the central legal body, the core of the FSF Europe. All members of the FSF Europe have to be members of the Hub by constitution.

Within the Hub the members from all European countries reach the European decisions together and agree on the course, policy and strategic issues of the FSF Europe.

These are then carried into the local legal bodies, the chapters through the constitution and the people.

The Chapters

The chapters are the local legal bodies of the FSF Europe, formed by the members of the FSF Europe from that country.

They are bound to the FSF Europe through their constitutions, but with the freedom and autonomy to address the local issues in the way appropriate for the cultural and social identity in those countries.

The constitution for these chapters is usually found by modifying a "template constitution" in order to fit in with the legal system of that country, aiming for charitable status whenever possible.
