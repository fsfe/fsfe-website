FSFE summit 2016 - September 2nd to 4th, BCC Berlin, Germany

Imagine a European Union that builds its IT infrastructure on Free Software. Imagine European Member States that exchange information in Open Standards and share their software. Imagine municipalities and city councils that benefit from decentralized and collaborative software under free licenses. Imagine no European is any longer forced to use non-Free Software.

This is what we are seeking. And although this vision feels like a long road to go, we know that we are taking major steps along it today. To help unlock our full potential on this road, FSFE offers cross-border collaboration and its first European summit. Be part of it and join our movement.

The conference program

You find the full conference program with detailes description at qtcon.org. Here, instead, you find a broad overview to get it at a glance.

Friday: All about business

The first day of the FSFE summit we are inviting business to share experiences or find collaborations to run their business with or on top of Free Software

Our speakers and topics in alphabetical order:

  • Steffen Evers: Open Source as a strategic tool
  • Peter Ganten: Obstacles for Open Source in SME's
  • and so on

Saturday: Community and Politics

Saturday is a day dedicated to Free Software policies and how to change them and to communities and how to organise them. Also, there will be our birthday celebration on Saturday.

Our speakers and topics in alphabetical order:

  • Mirko Boehm The emergence of governance norms in FLOSS communities
  • Dimitar Dimitrov Liquid Lobbying - Efficient advocacy for free and open
  • and so on

Sunday: Beyond Code

Sunday is dedicated to everything beyond code. Philosophy, universal access, human rights ... and how they interact with Free Software and its principles.

Our speakers and topics in alphabetical order:

  • Roberto Di Cosmo Software Heritage - the Universal Archive of Free Software
  • Jona Azizaj - Women in Open Source
  • and so on

The venue

The first FSFE summit will take place in the Berlin Congress Center as part of the QtCon16. For 3 days we will share the BCC together with VLC, KDE, KDAB and Qt. This brings some complex logistics with it but be assured that we do our best to let run everything as smooth as you expect it.
