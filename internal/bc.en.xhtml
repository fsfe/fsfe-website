Request Business Cards

"Business cards must answer the following quetions:
Who is this?
Who is represented?
What is he/she doing there?
How can I reach him/her?"
(Matthias Kirschner)

The purpose of this form is to avoid unnecessary, duplicate work. In the past, the process "production of business cards" triggered a minimum of 10 emails (but often enough far more) per person per set of cards. We want to reduce this. We do not assume that people are stupid. We do assume that some people simply do not know certain things. We do not assume, but we are sure, that Murphy is out there to get us.

Generally

Please write an expense request first.

The question whether you get cards or not, should be coordinated with the others (whoever that might be). FIXME: Pointer to a policy, whenever it might exist.

The details of your card (especially the title/function) should be coordinated with the appropiate Co-ordinator(s) to fit the FSFE's policy(s).

Please write an expense request first.

Language

English is default. If you need cards in another language for good reasons, we have to produce two sets of cards. Please use the form below twice in this case.

Content of the card

Please wait for the approval of your expense request before using that form.

1. First name and surname

We do use the international form with all parts in lower cases with a leading upper case for each word. If there are very good reasons to use another form, i.e. printing the surname in upper cases, as it is common in France, this should be done at the localised version of the cards, not in the int. version.
Data might include middle initials, but no nicknames as to be used in IRC. 
Joe Average
Joe M. L. Average
Joe Martin Average
Dr. Joe Average
Joe "Coolface" Average
Joseph AVERAGE
Average, Joseph

2. Your title (or function)

A title/function should be mentioned. It might be generic, somehow, i.e. "German Team".
In case of interns that might be a simple "intern", but you can leave it empty as well, for everything else than producing 500 cards has no use, and interns most likely tend to stay active in FSFE after their internship. 
Coordinator of $group
Intern
$country team
If you have two titles (or functions), both might be mentioned.

3. The line "Free Software Foundation Europe"

Don't care. This will be included automatically. 		Free Software Foundation Europe

4. Address

This should be the official FSFE address, wherever that might be. If you are working at an FSFE office, use this address, not your home address.
If there are very good reasons to use another address, please ask the others (whoever that might be). Do not forget to mention the country, please. 		Linienstraße 141, 10115 Berlin, Germany
Talstraße 110, 40127 Düsseldorf, Germany

5. Tel. (optional)

When working at an FSFE office, the number of that office should be mentioned. If not, it might be your own number or left empty. If in doubt, ask the others. The number should be grouped by two or three digits, seperated by spaces. 		p + 
p +49 30 275 95 290

6. Mobile (optional)

Your personal mobile no., e.g.
You should be reachable by phone. So as a minimum 5. or 6. should be fullfilled. 		m + 
m +49 30 275 95 290

7. Fax (optional)

It seems to be consensus in FSFE that this is an endangered communication protocoll. If you really are in need to use it, mention it by using the format of phone. (I can't remember having ever seen a fax number on a FSFE business cards...) 		f + 
f +49 30 203 405 60

8. Email

This should be your fsfe.org address (or fsfeurope.org, if everything else fails). 		E-mail 
another123@freemailservice.com

9. Jabber (optional)

Your FSFE jabber address. (You don't know that you have a jabber address? Something went wrong...) 		Jabber

10. Fingerprint

The fingerprint of your GnuPG key. No, not the key ID, not the kind of your key. The fingerprint will be printed in two lines.
(You do not have a GnuPG key? Something went terribly wrong.) 
2F77 A3E7 70E5 C802 2295
87D6 B8B7 8D13 B4BA 019E

(This is a theoretical example. It won't work!)

11. URL

There is no need to mention an URL. "www.fsfe.org" will be printed on the back side automatically. 		www.fsfe.org

12. Amount of cards

500 is the default value. 500 cards will cost some 32 EUR. Your expense request is approved? Everything less than 500 cards will cost almost the same. If there are good reasons to produce another amount, tell here.

13. No of approved expense request

You should have ensured that the cards are paid by FSFE. Please enter the number of the approved expense request here.

What happens now?

14. Preview

The printing company won't start the production before a preview (PDF) is approved by you. We will send it to you and will await your approval. We will use your fsfe.org address for that purpose. (If that won't work, you have a serious problem.)

15. Delivery address

After your approval, we will send the cards to the address which is printed on the cards, by default.
If you want us to send them elsewhere, tell us the other address, please.
If all needed data is available and correct, and you are fast by approving, you will receive your cards within 5 to 10 days, depending on the snail mail systems. Our all-time record is 24 hours. If problems are occuring, the delay will be hellish. Our all-time record is 6 months.
In most cases, office DUS can't answer your questions regarding the data. In seldom cases, we can. If in doubt, ask the others first.
