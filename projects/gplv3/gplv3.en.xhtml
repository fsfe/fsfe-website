GPLv3: Drafting version 3 of the GNU General Public License

A year-long public consultation is in progress for the drafting of version three of the GNU General Public License. Free Software Foundation Europe is working to raise awareness of the proposed changes and to maximise public participation in the process. If you have a suggestion for information which should be here, please contact us.

Introduction: What's happening

On January 16th 2006, Free Software Foundation (FSF), our sister organisation in the United States, published the first discussion draft of the GPL version 3 and launched a public consulation process which will last approximately one year.

At the end of this, FSF will publish version three of the GNU General Public License. This licence, commonly called "the GPL", is by far the most used licence for Free Software.

The task of the GPL is to ensure that everyone who receives a copy of GPL-licenced software is free to use that software, to modify it, and to redistribute it. The number of people who use software which is distributed under the terms of the GPL is somewhere in the region of 100s of millions.

Version 2 of the GPL was published in 1991. Version 3 will do the same job as version 2 but will be updated to account for changes in software's legal and technical environment, and to thwart new techniques devised in the past fifteen years to take the above freedoms away from software users.

How to participate

The public consultation is being managed by our sister organisation at the official GPLv3 website. There you can:

  • View existing comments
  • Sign-up for an account
  • Log-in
  • Submit your comments (after logging in)

By signing up, you will also be added to an announcement mailing list and will receive infrequent notifications of key GPLv3 events.

Timeline

The following is a timeline for the process with added notes about European GPLv3 events.

2006 January 16th
First public discussion draft is published, along with a rationale document explaining the changes. This marks the beginning of the public consultation process. This occurred during the first international GPLv3 conference.
2006 February 25th
Belgium: Richard Stallman gives a presentation on GPLv3 at FOSDEM (transcript online)
2006 March 18th
Italy: Richard Stallman gives a presentation on GPLv3 in Torino, at an Italian GPLv3 Conference (transcript online)
2006 April 21st & 22nd
2nd international GPLv3 conference, FISL, Porto Alegre, Brazil (transcript of Richard Stallman's presentation online)
2006 May 29th
Georg Greve spoke in Manchester: "Update of the constitution: introduction to GPLv3". For more information, see that event's external page.
2006 June 22nd & 23rd
The Third International GPLv3 Conference, in Barcelona, Spain.
2006 June 28th, 16h00
Ciaran O'Riordan will speak at GUADEC on "GPLv3 and free software development"
2006 July (approximate)
Second public discussion draft published, responding to seven months of public comments and review.
2006 August 22nd
Jonas Öberg will speak about GPLv3 at Dataföreningen Region West, Göteborg, Sweden
2006 August 23rd & 24th
Fourth International GPLv3 Conference, in Bangalore, India.
2006 August 24th
Jonas Öberg and Mathias Klang will speak about GPLv3 at Dataföreningen Region East, Linköping, Sweden
2006 October (approximate)
Fifth International GPLv3 Conference, in the Far East.
2007 January 15th
Probable release date for the official GPLv3
2007 March
Latest date for release of the official GPLv3
