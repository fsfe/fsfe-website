Free Sofware activists ask to the Italian Authority for the protection of personal data to publish readable documents.

FSFE, together with other nineteen Italian organisations, wrote a letter to the President of the Authority for the protection of personal data, Antonello Soro, to signal a readability problem of a document published on the website of the Authority. This document, burdened by scripts making it unreadable with Free Software, forces citizens to use a particular proprietary program. That is an obstacle to the freedom of choice by users, and it causes a bad episode of market distortion.

Beyond the Pdfreaders campaign

The PDFreaders campaign gave during last years excellent results. Italy particularly distinguished itself for the high number of signaled institutions and solved bugs, but the italian PDFReaders campaign doesn't stop. Italian Fellows discovered on the Authority website a PDF document unreadable for Free Software users. In this document, the use of a particular PDF reader is advertised. Together with the supporting organisations, FSFE decided to write a letter to the Authority for the protection of Personal data, to point out how this behaviour isn't neutral, and that some of the document on the institutional website aren't readable for Free Software users.
