France 

  * Status :      INITIAL PROJECT LAW
                  The ministry of culture is in charge of the translation

  * Law project : http://www.planetelibre.org/main.php?type=news

  * Pressbook : http://www.planetelibre.net/article/index.php?article=CSPLA

  * Documents : Oct 2001, CSPLA discuss action on the directive. They are expecting the project from
                the ministry of culture. Claims that professionals will be consulted by the ministry
                essentially thru the CSPLA itself. It analyses what should be translated (page 11, 12).
                It mentions that 5.1 (transmission and usage reproduction exceptions will need to be
                translated, without further explanations, presumably to modify CPI L122-6).
                http://www.culture.fr/culture/cspla/cr41001.pdf

                Dec 2001, CSPLA recommendations on the translation. 5.1 should be translated as-is (page 12).
                Protection mesures (page 13): it includes an important statement that says "identifiant les actes illicites, l'acte de contournement par toute personne des lors que le comportement litigieux est intentionnel" that should be stressed as much as possible to avoid a translation that opens the way to trial on the mere basis of the fact that a "mesure de contournement" exists. It is also added that private copy must be possible and that the author (or editor) does not prevent this by technical mesures. That is another idea to prevent a harmful translation. It also stresses the fact that the directive leaves room for purely contractual regulation for "on demand" publication (e-book for one).
                http://www.culture.fr/culture/cspla/corendu201201.pdf


                More recent reports do not show any indication of more precise works. In particular there
                are no suggested translation for the protection of technical measures.

  * CSPLA News : http://www.culture.fr/culture/cspla/comptes.htm

  * Ministere de la culture : http://www.droitsdauteur.culture.gouv.fr/

  * Report that may be in favor of a less protective law: http://www.videomuseum.fr/magnt/magnt4/713-lecm.htm

  * Local contact : APRIL loic@gnu.org

  * Agenda : Dec 5, 2002 the CSPLA will work on the project law.

             Dec 22, 2002 the dead line. It may be met. We cannot expect that the tranlation will take 5 years,
             there is enormous pressure.
