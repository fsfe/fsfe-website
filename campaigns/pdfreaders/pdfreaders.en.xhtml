Free Software PDF readers

Governments should not encourage their citizens to use proprietary software. Neither should they demand that citizens use one application alone when comparable alternatives exist. Yet many government websites do exactly this every day, especially in the field of PDF readers as you can see in the screenshots below.

Goal of the campaign

The goal of the campaign is to find out how much non-free pdf software advertisement is done on government websits and call their attention to this. When they make advertisement, they should advertise Free Software PDF readers because it allows people to be independent of a single vendor.

How to participate

By reporting proprietary PDF advertisements delivered by your government, we can encourage public organisations into removing endorsements of proprietary software.

From the 13th of September to the 17th October we ask you to hunt down Government websites that advertise proprietary PDF readers and report them using the form below.

You can also sign our Petition For The Removal Of Proprietary Software Advertising On Government Websites by ticking the box below.

The three best individual hunters will win a free entry to FSCONS, the world's premier conference for social change in technology taking place in Gothenburg from November 5th to November 7th 2010. Additionally, the winning team and the individual winners will receive a special FSFE gift box at the end of October.

Report an institution

Remark: You can participate in the hunt either as an individual or as a group. Groups will need to register by email to pdfreaders@lists.fsfe.org
