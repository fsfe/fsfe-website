GPLv3: Drafting version 3 of the GNU General Public License

Welcome to FSFE's GPLv3 project coordination page. Our goals are to raise awareness of the proposed changes, to help people to participate in the public consultation, and to produce educational materials to help people to make informed contributions.

Much of FSFE's GPLv3 work is made possible by the Fellowship of FSFE. To support the Fellowship, consider joining and encouraging others to do likewise.

Introduction: What's happening

On January 16th, 2006, a year-long public consultation process was launched for the drafting of version 3 of the GNU General Public License. On that day, the first public discussion draft was published, and the gplv3.fsf.org web portal went on-line to collect public comments.

On July 27th, the second draft was published, responding to six months of public comments. You can look at the changes from draft 1 to draft 2, or the changes from GPL version 2 to draft 2 of GPLv3.

Version 2 of the GNU GPL - the current official (non-draft) version - was published in 1991. The purpose of the GPL is to ensure that everyone who receives a copy of GPL-licenced software is free to use that software, to modify it, and to redistribute it in modified or unmodified form.

Today it is by far the most used licence for Free Software. The number of people who use software which is distributed under the terms of the GPL is somewhere in the region of 100s of millions.

Version 3 will do the same job as version 2 but will be updated to account for changes in software's legal and technical environment, and to thwart new techniques devised in the past fifteen years to take the above freedoms away from software users.

How to participate

The public consultation is being managed by our sister organisation at the official GPLv3 website. There you can:

  • View existing comments
  • Sign-up for an account
  • Log-in
  • Submit your comments (after logging in)

By signing up, you will also be added to an announcement mailing list and will receive infrequent notifications of key GPLv3 events.

Timeline

The following is the timeline for the milestones in the licence revision process.

For a list of GPLv3 events, see the Events page on the GPLv3 wiki.

2006 January 16th
First public discussion draft is published, the gplv3.fsf.org web portal goes online, and the public consultation process begins. This occurred during the first international GPLv3 conference.
2006 April 21st & 22nd
2nd international GPLv3 conference, FISL, Porto Alegre, Brazil (transcript of Richard Stallman's presentation online)
2006 June 22nd & 23rd
The 3rd International GPLv3 Conference, in Barcelona, Spain.
2006 July 27th
Second public discussion draft published, responding to six months of public comments and review.
2006 August 23rd & 24th
4th International GPLv3 Conference, in Bangalore, India.
2006 October (approximate)
Fifth International GPLv3 Conference, in the Far East.
2006 October (approximate)
A third public discussion draft.
2007 January 15th
Probable release date for the official GPLv3
2007 March
Latest date for release of the official GPLv3
