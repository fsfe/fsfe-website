FSFE Legal General FAQ

What is the Freedom Task Force?

The Freedom Task Force is FSFE Legal deparment, and we provide licence support services to individuals, projects and businesses involved with Free Software.

What is the structure of the Freedom Task Force?

We have a coordinator and a volunteer network containing people from multiple backgrounds.

What services does the Freedom Task Force offer?

We offer licensing services to individuals, projects and businesses involved with Free Software. These services include licence consultancy and education, fiduciary services and licence enforcement.

Do you just help with European projects?

We can help with worldwide licensing questions. If we cannot answer your questions directly we will help connect you to the right parties. One of our primary roles is to facilitate effective networking.

How can I contact the Freedom Task Force?

By emailing legal@fsfeurope.org

What connection do you have with gpl-violations.org?

We work in partnership with gpl-violations.org. Our primary area of cooperation is in dealing with violations of Free Software licences.

What connection do you have with the FSF License Lab?

All of the Free Software Foundations cooperate to ensure the promotion and protection of Free Software. A practical example of this would be that a recent licence violation case was passed from the FTF to the FSF Compliance Lab because it involved FSF copyright.

What connection do you have with the Software Freedom Law Center?

We have the same goal of strengthening the Free Software eco-system. We will cooperate where possible to ensure the promotion and protection of Free Software.
