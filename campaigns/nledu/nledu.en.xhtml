Our Work

Campaignname

The adoption of Free Software by schools and educational institutions is fundamental for our society. Following a campaign that started in the Netherland, where a crucial debate is going on at the political level, we wish to support the adoption of Open Standards and Free Software by all public-funded institutions in Europe, sharing the same objectives of our Dutch partners. Schools should teach children to be good members of the community, and should not provide product training for companies that do not respect freedom.

Our arguments:

  • We aim to make the use of open standard mandatory for all the public-funded institutions. Only open standards can assure a truly democratic and reliable access to information in the long periode.
  • We stand for the mandatory use in public institutions of platform-independent access to services and information. Proprietary services can force students and their families to buy and use proprietary software.
  • We struggle to stop the practice of free training for producers of proprietary software in public schools through the teaching of limited product specific skills. Free software allow student to share their software and to study how it works. It eliminates wage distinctions among different students: that's democracy! Furthermore, it helps teachers to teach values as tolerance and solidarity. The adoption of proprietary software in schools teaches students to be dependent from proprietary software producers. Free software allows them to understand the value of freedom.
  • We strive to stop the practice of selling software to teachers, students and their families against prices that are exremely below market prices.

In order to make sure this subjects are included in the European political agenda, and make sure that important policy change take place, we need to encourage public awareness at any level of the society. Your help is crucial.
