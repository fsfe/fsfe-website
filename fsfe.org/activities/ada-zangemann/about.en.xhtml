About the Book

Story & Themes

The illustrated book "Ada & Zangemann - A Tale of Software, Skateboards and Raspberry Ice Cream" tells the story of the famous inventor Zangemann and the girl Ada, a curious tinkerer. Ada begins to experiment with hardware and software, and in the process realises how crucial it is for her and others to control technology. Here the short summary:

The famous inventor Zangemann lives in a huge villa high above the city. Adults and children alike love his inventions and are desperate to have them. But then something happens: when Zangemann wants to take another close-up look at his inventions during a walk through the city, a child hits him in the shin with the skateboard. That hurts! Enraged, the inventor makes a momentous decision... The clever girl Ada sees through what is going on. Together with her friends, she forges a plan.

A book that arouses children's interest in tinkering and encourages shaping technology. From age 6 to 106.

Author & Illustrator

Matthias Kirschner is the author of the book and president of the FSFE. Matthias has always been fascinated by computers and software. From a very early age, he had an internet connection, which taught him a great deal. Children have the right to take control of their own future. Matthias is therefore committed to the goal of enabling all people to use software in a self-determined way. He also wants to build an electric scooter using Free Software.

Sandra Brandstätter is the book’s illustrator. Sandra has always loved doing crafts with scissors, pliers, glue, wire, fabric and paper. Today she works as an illustrator, comic book author and character designer for films and animated series, such as the German public television series ‘Trudes Tier’. If she had her pick from an ice cream machine, Sandra would choose a creamy rainbow ice cream with cucumber and lemon flavour and plenty of sprinkles.
