Internships

FSFE is a charitable non-profit organisation dedicated to promoting Free Software. We are working to build freedom in the digital society. We operate in a lively environment of numerous volunteers from many countries and several full-time employees.

Working at the Free Software Foundation Europe offers to get close to an amazing variety of topics and tasks. This diversity brings a lot of technical, organisational, social and political experience and skills, which are great to have for any Free Software enthusiast. Nicolas Jean, Current intern, Webmaster

FSFE is a stimulating environment to work in. There are few organisations where you can learn from so many perspectives in this international and interdisciplinary context. If you read my blog, you can have a good overview of some things I've been working on. Hugo Roy, Intern 2009-2010, Fellowship representative 2011

FSFE is looking for smart, motivated interns to join our team. We offer

  • a truly challenging and exciting time with a dynamic NPO working internationally,
  • a close-up view of organisational and community processes,
  • a chance to take the initiative and put your ideas into practice,
  • the opportunity to meet and work with Free Software advocates across Europe.

Key responsibilities:

  • contributing to FSFE ongoing projects,
  • work on campaigns and public relations,
  • communicating with contacts from the FSFE community, NPO, industry, and public administrations,
  • coordinating volunteers and FSFE team members during work on various projects,
  • general office tasks,
  • finding your own strengths.

Basic internship facts:

  • Duration: 4-12 months (full-time).
  • Starting date: anytime.
  • Location: Berlin, Germany.
  • Remuneration: EURO 400/month.

Knowledge, experience, skills:

  • solid basic knowledge of, and considerable interest in, Free Software (required),
  • excellent knowledge of English (written and spoken) is a requirement; a working knowledge of German and other languages is a plus,
  • basic acquaintance with working on GNU/Linux systems (desirable),
  • administration and management skills,
  • ability to being creative, multitasking,responsible,
  • ability to integrate into a young, motivated and professional team,
  • no programming skills required.

How to apply:

Interested? Please send us a mail with your letter of motivation, a CV and references (in English) to office@fsfeurope.org. Please do not send any documents in Microsoft Word document format.

Interns must have a valid visa to enter Germany (a residence permit to stay here during intenship) and take out their own health insurance before starting the internship.

Applications are considered on a rolling basis. FSFE will be keeping a record of all applications and invite potential candidates for interviews as soon as an opening is approaching.

We are looking forward to receiving your application!
