Speakers

If you would like to invite a speaker who can represent the FSF Europe and/or the Free Software Movement at a conference, event or panel discussion, you can use this page to find someone to speak on behalf of the FSF Europe and/or the Free Software Movement. Speakers are listed in alphabetical order by last name.
If you would like to invite one of these speakers to an event, please contact the speaker directly via email or contact us.

List of speakers

  • Frédéric Couchet
  • Loïc Dachary
  • Georg C. F. Greve

Frédéric Couchet

Home Page: http://www.april.org/~mad/
Email: FredericCouchet@fsfeurope.org

Biography

Frédéric Couchet is the founding president of APRIL and works in a Free Software company. He is also a founding member and French Chancellor of the FSF Europe.

Topics

Frédéric Couchet can address the following topics:

  • Presentation of the history and concepts of the Free Software.
  • Presentation and role of APRIL
  • Operation, actions and future of the Free Software Foundation Europe
  • Software patents

Languages

  • French

Loïc Dachary

Home Page: http://www.dachary.org/loic/
Email: loic@gnu.org

Biography

Loïc first got involved in Free Software in 1987. He is now a senior developer who contributed to many software, and is the lead of Senga (development group for information retrieval software). In January 2001 he initiated Savannah to improve the infrastructure of the GNU project. He is also a former vice president of the FSF Europe.

Topics

Loïc Dachary can address the following topics:

  • Development of Free Software
  • Actions and future of FSF Europe
  • The infrastructure and dynamics of the GNU Project
  • Free Software solutions for information retrieval

Languages

  • French
  • English

Georg C. F. Greve

Home Page: http://greve.gnuhh.org/
Email: mailto:Greve@fsfeurope.org

Biography

Georg C. F. Greve is a Physicist (German Diploma from the University of Hamburg) with experience in medical science, physical oceanography, biophysics and nanotechnology areas although computer science has always been a focal point. Example activities are his authorship of the monthly GNU forum "Brave GNU World" and the GNU Xlogmaster. He is also initiator and president of the FSF Europe. More information is available at his home page.

Topics

Georg C. F. Greve can speak about the following topics:

  • Visions for Free Software and the FSF
  • Background, concept and future of the FSF Europe
  • History, background and philosophy of Free Software and the GNU Project
  • "Copyright in the internet age": Implications for copyright by the digital revolution
  • Commercial, practical and sociological aspects of Free Software and the GNU Project
  • The "Brave GNU World"

Languages

  • German (native)
  • English (near-native)
