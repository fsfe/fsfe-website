Arouse children's -- especially girls -- joy to tinker with technology

The FSFE has created the illustrated book "Ada & Zangemann - A Tale of Software, Skateboards, and Raspberry Ice Cream" under an Open Education License (like Wikipedia). This has enabled people around the world to use the materials to encourage children to tinker with hardware and software and encourage them to shape their own technology.

Readings in schools, libraries, cinemas, ...

Our volunteers, staff, and the author of the book have done many readings in different languages throughout the world. They have read the story of the famous inventor Zangemann and the girl Ada, a curious tinkerer. Ada begins to experiment with hardware and software, and in the process realises how crucial it is for her and others to control technology. By reading the book to over 1000 children in the last months we got the experience that children and especially girls afterwards want to get into "inventing things" and programming.

For the events we have collaborated with organisations that introduce children into programming, with an embassy to organise a reading for refugee children including gifting them books, and brining an ice cream machine to the reading so the children could enjoy different ice cream afterwards.

Accessibility - Translations and alternative versions

Our goal is to make the materials accessible for everyone. Thanks to donations, the FSFE has paid for translations of the book into Arabic and Ukrainian, the French Ministry of Educations has coordinated the French translation and publication, an automotive company commissioned a translation into Spanish, and volunteers have translated the book into Valencian, Danish, Hindi, Italian, and are working on additional translations. Thereby enabling people to access the story in their native language.

Furthermore we are working with organisations and publishers on accessible ebook versions for people that read differently, due to disabilities like blindness or low vision and will make them available in those formats.

Wider distribution with video / audio version

To reach more children, we will create a simple video and audio version of the book produced by an experienced 2D animation studio, also published as an Open Educational Resource. Everyone will be able free to download and share the video, put it on their or the schools computers, ship it with other education material, embed it on company's and organisation's websites, or display the video at events.

If you make a donation to the FSFE and want to be mentioned in the announcement of the videos, write us a short e-mail why you support the FSFE's work and we will thank you by name in the announcement. If you donate more than 480 Euros you will be mentioned in the credits.
