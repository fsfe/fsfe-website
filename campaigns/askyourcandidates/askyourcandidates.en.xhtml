Our Work

"Ask your candidates"

"Ask Your Candidates" is an ongoing activity where you can always help. We encourage you to ask your local, regional, national candidates or parties where they stand regarding Free Software and connected topics. We maintain a list of upcoming elections. Take a look at it to see if you can do something in your area, and help us to keep it up to date.

What to ask?

Before you start asking questions you should think about what you want to ask. We encourage you to dive into our different areas of activies, our news items, and take a look at already asked questions as an inspiration for your own questions.

How to contact candidates?

Contacting candidates

  • Identify candidates working at the level where the election is taking place who are likely to respond to the question (have already tackled the issue, have worked in the area…)
  • Send them the questions
  • Publish the reply and report it to us.

Contacting the parties

  • Create a survey , print it and send it to the relevant parties.
  • Summarize the position of the parties you contacted on a wiki page.

How to create awareness around your action ?

  • Inform us that you want to start an action for this campaign.
  • Create a wiki page (two examples under parliamentary elections in Germany 2009 and comunal election in Vienna 2010) and list:
    • The position of the parties involved (the result of the survey you've sent them)
    • The questions that you or your group have asked and the answers you've received
  • Blog regularly about your action: when you start contacting candidates, what you do, when you receive an answer, etc…. Use also microblogging to give quick updates on where you're standing in the process.
