1 Upcycling Android

Why should I upcycle my phone?

Do you find yourself changing your smartphone device too often? It is not you, it is often the software of the device that makes it unusable in a short time. Instead of buying a new device, reclaim your smartphone by choosing Free Software. Save yourself some money and protect the environment. Upcycle your Android.

The current situation

We need to deal with technology in a sustainable way. Let's start with one of the most vital piece of technology in our daily life: the smartphone. How does our current behaviour with smartphones affect the environment?

1. We replace our smartphones too often.

Smartphones are too valuable devices, to be replaced every other year or less.

2. Tons of discarded phones damage the environment.

E-waste increases every year. In 2019 alone over 50 million tons of electronic waste have globally been disposed. Recycling is scarce, and most of the scrap ends up in the countries of the global south.

3. Producing smartphones requires a lot of energy.

It takes a lot of energy to produce smartphones. In fact, producing the device takes as much energy as using your phone for four years. This means that when you buy a new phone, considerable environmental damage has already happened to produce it. And the sooner we waste a device, the more we need to produce.

The silver lining

The current situation can be drastically improved.

1. Even when the software lags, hardware remains in good condition.

You may decide to change your device for many reasons. The device becomes slow or non-responsive, or the manufacturing company stops supporting its software, which leads to security risks. In most of these cases the hardware still works pretty well.. So why tossing away your shiny expensive hardware? Wouldn't it be better instead to update and keep using it? That is what upcycling is about!

2. The software can be replaced.

Smartphones can be modified. An operating system is a collection of software that enables you to use your phone and run applications on it. You do not need to stick to the default operating system. Instead, you can install an alternative operating system on your phone. For some devices, this process is simple.

3. A device can become faster if it runs Free Software.

How many apps were already on your phone the first time you turned it on? How many of them are you actually using? How many of them run advertisements you are not interested in? Could you uninstall unwanted apps? Pre-installed software often exhausts the battery and processing power of your device. Switch to Free Software and take back control of your apps. Only install the apps you like and you will see your phone operating better than before.
