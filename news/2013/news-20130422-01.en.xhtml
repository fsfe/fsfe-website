German Parliament says: Stop Granting Software Patents

The German Parliament, the Bundestag, has passed a joint motion against software patents. The resolution urges the German government to take steps to limit the granting of patents on computer programs.

"Software patents are harmful in every way, and make life unnecessarily difficult for developers and companies", says Karsten Gerloff, President of the Free Software Foundation Europe. "We urge the German government to act on this resolution as soon as possible, and relieve German software businesses from the needless patent-related costs and risks under which they are currently suffering."

The Parliament has resolved that software should be covered by copyright exclusively, ensuring that developers have the right to release their work as Free Software.

software patents are illegal under the European Patent Convention. Nevertheless, the European Patent Office has granted tens of thousands of patents on software.
