Get Involved

Read it to others

One way to raise awareness of software freedom is to organise a book reading and spread its message. If you organise a reading, we recommend that it takes place in a location with few distractions. Readings in "open spaces" turned out to be more challenging for the reader, and especially younger children are way more distracted by noise or movement in another area of the room. For smaller readings, try to create a cosy environment so not to have too much space between you and the audience. For example, making the audience sit on the floor on pillows, with the reader in front of them. You can find everything you need to organise a reading in this repository.

The author Matthias Kirschner has already given several readings of the book: At events for children and adults (from 6 to 106 years), in school classes, libraries and on other occasions. If you are are interested in a reading by the author or in other available languages by the FSFE volunteers, please contact us at contact@fsfe.org.

Share your thoughts with others

A picture says more than a thousand words! You can use sharepic generator to show your love about the book and post your review by generating a picture that you can share on social media channels or add to your blog.

Gift the book to others

Giving the book as a gift is a great way to support it. You can buy a copy to give as a gift at your favorite bookstore or online.

Suggest to Libraries / Schools

You can suggest the book to your public library because they accept always recommendations of new books. Also, you can even donate the book, if a library does not have the budget to buy the book, but you can afford it. Your copy will make many people happy. In addition to public libraries, you can also provide feedback to bookstores about the book so they can include it in their catalog and recommend it to others. If you bought the book online, you can also write a review.

You can also offer schools or youth clubs to read the book there. They often ask for people reading books and it is a good way to talk about software freedom. On the resources page, you can find more information to have additional material of the book.

Include e-book and movies as example files in installations at schools, universities, etc.

Translate

The FSFE would like as many people as possible to learn about the book and being able to read it in their mother tongue. If you would like to do a community translation, please let the FSFE's translation team know about it and have a look at the translation guidelines of the Git repository.

If you have any contacts with publishers, which are interested to publish the book in another language, or if you are interested to order a higher amount of books if the book would be available in a certain language, then please reach out to us.

Adapt

Help with automation

The impact of the story is increased when the story is available in more languages and more formats. Therefore the automation should make it easy to add new translations and formats. It should also be easy to generate and finetune output files, regardless of technical skill or operating system. Free culture should be supported when selecting programs and content like fonts. If you'd like to help automate the book, you can check out the relevant documentation.

Become a FSFE Supporter

The Free Software Foundation Europe is a charity that empowers users to control technology. Software is deeply involved in all aspects of our lives, and it is important that this technology empowers rather than restricts us. Free Software gives everybody the rights to use, understand, adapt, and share software. These rights help support other fundamental freedoms like freedom of speech, press, and privacy.

The FSFE helps individuals and organisations to understand how Free Software contributes to freedom, transparency, and self-determination. To be an independent voice for Free Software, we depend on donations. With financial support you will contribute to a solid foundation on which we are building our charitable work for freedom in the information society.
