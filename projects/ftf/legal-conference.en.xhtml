The European Legal Network Conference

The European Legal Network Conference is held every year to allow legal experts to discuss the future of Free Software licenses and associated best practice in this field. This has become the world's premier event for debating, discussing and sharing knowledge around Free Software legal affairs. It has a strong focus on legal fact rather than opinion, the growing eco-system and internationalization. Attendees seek to explore and facilitate cooperative solutions for the largest common challenges.

The European Legal Network Conference 2011 will feature speakers like Eben Moglen from SFLC, Scott Peterson from HP, Jason Wacha from MontaVista and Harald Welte from gpl-violations.org. Our theme this year will be 'Free Software law for the next ten years.'

We will hold talks and panels over two days in a single track while also providing ample time for networking and free discussion. There will be a room dedicated to talks and panels, and a room dedicated to informal meetings. Both will be equipped with screens and video projectors. Coffee and tea will be served during the event, along with a buffet lunch and water throughout.
