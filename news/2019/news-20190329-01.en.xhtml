#ilovefs Day report 2019

On Wednesday 14th of February, many chose to acknowledge and celebrate the annual "I love Free Software Day". It was THE day to express your love for Free Software and gratitude for your favourite pieces of Free Software. The Free Software Foundation Europe would like to say a huge THANK YOU to everyone who joined and contributed to making this day special for everyone involved.

Loving Free Software #ilovefs

What actually happened in numbers and pictures

We counted hundreds of Tweets and Posts both on Twitter and the Fediverse, and we are happy to announce that participation has increased a lot compared to previous years Some were inspired to come up with their own visuals, while others decided to use our balloons and posters to decorate and remind those in the offline world the importance of thanking on this day all those Free Software solutions that they use on a daily basis:

Spreading love for Free Software offline #ilovefs

Wikimedia spreading love for Free Software in Berlin

People from all over the world joined with their own blog posts, news items, photos and artworks showing and encouraging others to join using the hashtag #ilovefs. As a result, Twitter, the Fediverse and even Facebook were flooded with love messages by people tagging their favourite Free Software projects. Thanks to our former intern and current volunteer Jan Weymeirsch who wrote a scraper to collect and analyse the data, accurately following each #ilovefs post, we have exact numbers and visuals to support them:

Participation in #ILoveFS on Social Media through time

Overall 667 distinct #ilovefs posts on Twitter and Fedeverse were counted, made by 530 individual accounts, mentioning at least 231 projects and developers. Posts on Twitter were coming from at least 230 different locations from all over the world in 16 different languages. There were many more "I love Free Software"; posts that did not use the hashtag #ilovefs.

#ilovefs participation on Twitter, Fediverse and Reddit

The important takeaway from all these numbers is the feeling of community that it generates; that such a diverse group of people can share common values and attitudes towards software. It shows how much we all love sharing, using, studying, and improving software for a better digital and analogue world!

#ilovefs panel by Zalando

Save the date!

In case you missed your opportunity to thank your favourite project, mark February 14 as "I Love Free Software Day" in your calendar for next year. But remember, you can always and without a special occasion create and opportunity to express your gratitude and appreciation throughout the entire year. You do not need a special day, as there is simply no wrong time for that!

We would like to thank everyone involved in and contributing to this day, as well as the countless developers, translators, community managers, artists and anyone involved in Free Software.

We hope that this year's "I love Free Software" Day has planted the seeds of Free Software passion in all of you, just like Sonja planted hers.
