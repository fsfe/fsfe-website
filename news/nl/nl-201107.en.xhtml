FSFE Newsletter - July 2011

Attack on Free Software and the GNU GPL

Imagine you bought a computer with pre-installed Free Software. After a while you decide to install some additional software made by someone else. The company that sold you your computer does not like this however, and decides to sue the people who made the additional software that you installed. Sounds like maybe buying your computer from that company was not such a good idea!

At the moment something very similar is happening in an important GNU GPL violation case in Germany, in which FSFE and gpl-violations.org are participating. AVM maintains in court that others should not be allowed to modify software on computers bought from them, including the Linux kernel. Although the Linux kernel is distributed under the GNU GPL which guarantees exactly this freedom to users.

AVM is attacking the very foundations of Free Software: They want to take away freedom from others. It will directly contravene the legal rights of the original authors of the programs who decided that software freedom and cooperation is more important to them than directly receiving license fees. If AVM is successful in court it will be disastrous for Free Software on embedded devices, including mobile phones, network hardware, and other Free Software based products.

The court made no decision during the last hearing on June 21st. The participants may yet file further written pleas. On 27th September the court will either make a ruling on the case directly, or choose to begin hearing evidence. FSFE and gpl-violations.org have published a detailed report about the case, and will continue to monitor it and defend the freedom of software users.

FSFE Internal: An era ends - others start

Usually you do not hear about the people who work behind the scenes for FSFE. This is because most of them are volunteers, and the press and publicity work is handled by people like our president Karsten Gerloff, or your editor. But those volunteers give up their spare time to promote software freedom, and without them FSFE would not be what it is today.

At FSFE's General Assembly which took place in Ljubljana, Slovenia, on June 11, FSFE's members elected Henrik Sandklef as Vice President. A computer scientist and GNU Hacker from Gothenburg, Sweden, Henrik has been active with FSFE since 2005. He takes over from Fernanda Weiden, who held the volunteer position for the past two years. The General Assembly also confirmed Karsten Gerloff as FSFE's President, and Reinhard Müller as Financial Officer.

During that weekend an era ended, as Bernhard Reiter, co-founder of FSFE, completed ten years as German coordinator and Deputy. He was the first FSFE representative to continuously hold positions for so long within the organisation, and also the only person to participate in all of FSFE's annual General Assemblies. Bernhard will remain active within FSFE, but has now handed the official post of Deputy German Coordinator to Torsten Grote.

The European Commission’s locked-in syndrome

It is official: The European Commission will migrate to Microsoft Windows 7 without considering alternative solutions. In a reply to questions asked by MEP Bart Staes (Greens/EFA), the European Commission confirmed that it has awarded contracts for the 'upgrade' to Microsoft and reseller Fujitsu-Siemens on behalf of 55 other European institutions and the Commission itself. As Karsten explains in his blog article, this move will drive the EC into even greater dependence on Microsoft.
