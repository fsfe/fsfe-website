Valentine's Day traditionally is a day to show and celebrate your love to whom you like to dedicate it to. But instead of solely showing your love to your beloved one, we think Valentine's Day can be a great opportunity to declare your love and show your gratitude to the people behind any and every Free Software initiative or organisation, too. This will not only delight the people behind your preferred Free Software - beyond that, you can support us in celebrating and establishing Valentines Day as an "I love Free Software"-Day.
Last years Valentine's Day was a big success for the "I love Free Software"-Day, thank you all for your support. If you like to know more about last years campaign, please have a look at the report of Valentine's Day 2011.

This years Valentine's Day, Tuesday 14th of February 2012, the FSFE is planning an online campaign and an offline event as well. Both will be dedicated to "I love Free Software".

Spread your love for Free Software: go public Online Campaigning

Behind every Free Software initiative and organisation are real, passionate hard-working people. Show them that you appreciate their work – it could make all the difference!

In fact, there are lots of ways to declare one's love... Here are some ideas on how you can go public and proclaim your love to Free Software.

  • Send active Free Software supporters a message thanking them for their work. Do not underestimate the effects of such a message. As many Free Software developers work hard for their projects without earning money, a nice "thank you" is a good currency for further encouragement
  • Especially on Valentine's Day, microblog about how you love Free Software, and use the Hashtag #ilovefs.
    • Encourage your friends to do as well, to reach a high ranking on Twitter trends and hashtag timelines in identi.ca and every other social blogging system.
  • If you are a Blogger, blog about the "I love Free Software"-Day and the idea behind it. Get people attracted by the idea of Free Software. Look for our Banners at the bottom of this page.
  • If you are living in Germany, promote our Offline-Event in the Unperfekt-Haus in Essen

Money can't buy you love...

… but it can help to fight for your freedom. Donate to FSFE or another Free Software initiative to express your gratitude. They depend on your contribution to continue their work. So check out your favourite organisation and make a donation. You can be sure they will love you back.

Show your love - Individually

  • Hug a developer (Obviously better to ask permission first ; )
  • Buy your favourite developer a drink. Or buy someone else a drink and while savouring it, tell him/her about your favorite Free Software application!
  • Send a person a Postcard to tell her about your love to Free Software. We will provide you some drafts in the next day here on this webpage.
  • Send a book to your favourite contributor and support FSFE at the same time.
  • Wear Free Software shirts and sweaters. If you need new stuff, you can order some from us, or from your favourite Free Software project.
  • Send us pictures of how you're expressing your feelings for Free Software. We'll publish them on our website, making a gallery of Free Software lovers from around the world!

Offline-Event: I love Free Software

This year for the first time, there will be an #iovefs-Offline-Event, too. This event will take place at the Unperfekthaus in Essen. We would like this event to bring together Free Software users, activists, developers and everyone else who is in favor of Free Software.
The Unperfekthaus in Essen seems best location to celebrate Free Software, as it is a building to host free minds at nearly no cost, to colaborate and elaborate their ideas and thougts - similar to many Free Software projects. We are still in progress of setting up our schedule, but here is a general explanation about how the day will be: When? 14/02/2012 - We will be situated the whole day in the Unperfekthaus How Much? Everybody who wants to join our event has to pay normal fee for the Unperfekthaus, but is allowed - of course - to leave our event and to visit the whole Unperfekthaus like usual paying includes. (Visit the homepage to learn more about the house and its possibilities hier) What to expect? Over the day we will offer some workshops and introductions to Free Software and its aspects. This will give a chance for newcomers to get to know what we are talking about when we say "Free as in Free Software". But there will be some discussions and informations for oldies too, about ongoing projects, unperfekt ideas and so on.
We tried to set everything this way up, that you can bring your family, too. There will be amusement for the kids as well as amusments for loving partnerships.
You like to contribute something? We will set up a speakers corner for everyone to do brief speeches, you can set up spontaneous workshops or discussion groups, feel free to present you own Free Software product or the one you love the most ... In the end and over all, there is the goal to celebrate together and have some fun, to get to know each other and to learn from each other ....
In the evening some bands will be playing live-music. These bands will be announced in next days.

Why they love Free Software

"On Valentines Day, the Society for Free Culture and Software, extends their love to projects such as Audacity, GIMP, Inkscape and other audiovisual tools. These tools form the backbone of the infrastructure needed to support free culture and while Free Culture could technically exist independently of Free Software, the two movements share a common philosophy of freedom which make the two inseparable. Without Free Software, our activities would be so much more difficult to carry out, and no Free Culture project would be able to enjoy the full freedoms without the infrastructure created by Free Software projects such as those outlined before. To everyone engaged in supporting this infrastructure: thank you! We love you!"
— Jonas Oberg, Society for Free Culture and Software (Föreningen fri kultur och programvara)
