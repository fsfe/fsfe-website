Education Task Force

Reasons to advocate for Free Software in your university

With Free Software, you have the choice. You are not stuck in one single distribution because you only know how to use this particular one, because if you change, the format of your documents will not be compatible with the new version, or because you have paid a license that holds you from modifying the software you are working on.

Free Software improves your education

-------------------------------------------------------

Acquiring displayable skills

Nowadays, it is not enough to know how to use a certain office program. It's important to understand the concepts underlying a whole category or type of software not merely how to use a particular application. Free Software permits you to learn how software works and thus to adapt to any environment, a key skill nowadays.

Being independent

If you are taught to rely on proprietary software, you are made dependent on something you'll have to pay for. If you are taught to rely on Free Software, you can continue using this software in your adult life while helping others by sharing it.

Learning in native language

Free Software applications are being translated into almost any language. Thus you can learn and focus on the actual subject without any language or cultural barriers.

Using Free Software is the logical economic decision

-------------------------------------------------------

Keep your university neutral

Universities have to stay away from consumerism. Universities' needs, and the means they require, have to keep clear from fashion or software industry advertising.

Spend money on grey matter

In Education, means are more limited than in other sectors, because they have to be employed on a large scale. It's more relevant to pay grey matter (e.g. help for projects conception, personal and educational training) than to pay proprietary licences.

Be ecologically responsible

Free Software can be used with minimal hardware requirements on almost any hardware. Free Software gives increased perennity to hardware as it allows a longer use of old (and cheap) hardware. Updates can be immediatly developed and shared with other users worldwide. You don't need to buy the last release of the producer.

Free Software contribute to building a free society

-------------------------------------------------------

Solidarity

Using Free Software allows you to share and cooperate and thus join a whole community that shares knowledge.

Liberty

Free Software is about freedom, not price. With Free Software, you have the control over the software you use. You can use, study, share and improve it.

Equality

With free software, teachers can give a copy to each student. Thus children of families with less financial resources can learn with the same tools as every other child
