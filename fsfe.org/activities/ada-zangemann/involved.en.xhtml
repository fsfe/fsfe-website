Get Involved

Read it to others

One way to spark children's interest in coding is to read the book to them: in your family, friend's or co-worker's families. It is very motivating and fulfilling to discuss the topic with children and hear about their ideas what they want to accomplish with technology. You can e.g. just download the presenation PDF slides and use the text with the markers when to turn the slides".

If you feel comfortable you can also organise a larger book reading. You can offer that to schools, youth clubs, or libraries. They often ask for people reading books and it is a good way to talk with children about their thoughts on technology and about software freedom. On the resources page, you can find additional material of the book.

If you organise a reading, we recommend that it takes place in a location with few distractions. Readings in "open spaces" turned out to be more challenging for the reader, and especially younger children are way more distracted by noise or movement in another area of the room. For smaller readings, try to create a cosy environment so not to have too much space between you and the audience. For example, making the audience sit on the floor on pillows, with the reader in front of them. You can find everything you need to organise a reading in our documentation .

The author Matthias Kirschner has already given several readings of the book: At events for children and adults (from 6 to 106 years), in school classes, libraries, cinemas, and on other occasions. If you are are interested in a reading by the author or in other available languages by the FSFE volunteers, please contact us at contact@fsfe.org.

Share your thoughts with others

A picture says more than a thousand words! You can use sharepic generator to show your love about the book and post your review by generating a picture that you can share on social media channels or add to your blog.

Gift the book to others

Gifting the book to friends and family or co-workers will help to spark children's interest in coding. You can buy a copy online or at your favourite bookstore. After buying it helps a lot if you leave a review or feedback towards the store, so they can recommend it to others as well.

If you would like to buy a larger amount of discounted books, e.g. to gift to employees, partners, or for events, please contact us at contact@fsfe.org.

Suggest to Libraries / Schools

You can recommend the book to your public library or you can even donate the book, if a library does not have the budget to buy the book, but you can afford it. The same applies to youth clubs, schools, or hackerspaces. Your copy will enable children to read it who might not have the financial means to buy it themselves.

Include e-book and movies as example files in installations at schools, universities, etc.

All the material around the book and the movie is available under Creative Commons CC By Share Alike. This enables you to include the ebook or the movie on computers at schools, universities, or as example files for GNU/Linux distributions. This is a great way to spread the story further. If you did, please let us know about it.

Organise a Screening

If reading the book aloud is not your thing, you could also organise a movie screening. Either in a private setting, or with the DCP movie files even renting a cinema and inviting families, your employees with children, or school classes to attend. For the German premiere we rented the largest cinema in Germany and watched the movie with over 800 children. This is perfect to spark children's interest in technology.

Translate

The FSFE would like as many people as possible to learn about the book and being able to read it in their mother tongue. If you would like to do a community translation you can directly start on our Weblate instance, please let the FSFE's translation team know about it and have a look at the translation guidelines.

If you have any contacts with publishers, which are interested to publish the book in another language, or if you are interested to order a higher amount of books if the book would be available in a certain language, then please contact us at contact@fsfe.org.

Adapt

In the spirit of the license, you can automatically generate book variants based on templates and the translated text yourself. You want to change names in the book to your children's name? You do not like some parts of the story and want to make changes? This practical guide explains how to build the various output formats. It is the same tooling that automatically builds different formats of the book from the translations on Weblate like the screen readable PDFs.

Help with automation

The impact of the story is increased when the story is available in more languages and more formats. Therefore the automation should make it easy to add new translations and formats. It should also be easy to generate and finetune output files, regardless of technical skill or operating system. Free culture should be supported when selecting programs and content like fonts. If you would like to help improving the tool chain for the story, you can check out the relevant documentation.

Become a FSFE Supporter

The Free Software Foundation Europe is a charity that empowers users to control technology. Software is deeply involved in all aspects of our lives, and it is important that this technology empowers rather than restricts us. Free Software gives everybody the rights to use, understand, adapt, and share software. These rights help support other fundamental freedoms like freedom of speech, press, and privacy.

The FSFE helps individuals and organisations to understand how Free Software contributes to freedom, transparency, and self-determination. To be an independent voice for Free Software, we depend on donations. With financial support you will contribute to a solid foundation on which we are building our charitable work for freedom in the information society.
