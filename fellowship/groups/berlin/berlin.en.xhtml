Fellowship Group Berlin

This is the page of a local Fellowship group of the Free Software Foundation Europe. Here you find all relevant information about who we are and how to contact us. No matter if you are already a Fellow or not - we are always happy about new people interested in Free Software.

Berlin Fellowship Group

About

Group Name: "Berlin Freedom"
Philosophy: "Hacking for freedom"

Founded in: 2007
Members: ~ 15
Coordinator(s): Erik Albers, Silvan Heintze

Description:

The Fellowship group in Berlin is a group of people that love Free Software and the idea of bringing people together that care about Software freedom as well as civil digital rights. We are not just hackers but political activists - online and offline.
Our meetings usually have an Agenda along that we discuss most important and recent topics. Sometimes we have a talk, sometimes we do certain acitivities, sometimes we support FSFE's campaigns. No matter if you are interested in joining our activities or only like to chat with likeminded people - you are welcome!
The meetings are open for public and you do not have to be or become a Fellow to join our meetings.

Meetings:

We meet regularly every second Thursday a month in the youth center "E-Lok" in Laskerstraße 6-8, 10245 Berlin. Meeting time begins at 7pm, official start of the Agenda is 7.30pm. There is good food available for little money, the same is true for drinks (alcoholic and non-alcoholic). At the same time there is a meeting of the local Linux User-Group "Linux-Works!" This leads to interesting interchanges from time to time.

Get in contact:

To get in contact with the Berlin Fellowship group you can contact Berlins coordinators directly (see above). Or you subscribe to the mailing list and contact the whole group by writing to the mailing list. To get more information about Berlins Fellowship group you can also browse our wiki-pages.
