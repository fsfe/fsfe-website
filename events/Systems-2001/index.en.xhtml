FSF Europe on the Systems 2001

The FSF Europe was present at the Systems 2001 in Munich, Germany with a booth in the Free Software community corner of the "GNU/LinuxPark."

Monday, October 15th, 2001:

Here are some pictures taken by Klaus Kappel, one of our booth volunteers:


The FSF Europe booth is right next to Debian GNU/Linux.


Volker Dormeyer, our booth coordinator and organizator for the Systems, he is the one who made it possible for us to be here.


Georg Greve gives a presentation on "Copyright in the Internet Age" (in German).

Tuesday, October 16th, 2001:

Pictures courtesy of Felix Nenz (of the FFII):


Volker put up the logo that we got printed thanks to the support of Simon Budig (GIMP) and Kurt Pfeifle (CUPS). Free Software cooperation at work.


As you can see, someone "patched" the plan by now...


Bernhard Reiter gives a presentation on "How companies can profit from Free Software philosophy" (in German).

Wednesday, October 17th, 2001:

Bernhard Reiter gives his presentation "How companies can profit from Free Software philosophy" (in German) a second time.
Interview of Georg Greve by it-tv (http://www.it-tv.de) about the FSF Europe, the GNU Project and Free Software. Unfortunately not available in a Free format at the moment, so here are some still photos:

Thursday, October 18th, 2001:

Podium discussion about "10 years of Linux." Moderator: Jan Kleinert (editor, Linux Magazin). Participants: Hans Baier (Caldera Germany), Robin Tawa (IT Consultant, formerly Apptime), Udo Hertz (IBM EMEA), Georg Greve (president FSF Europe), Sebastian Hetze (Linux Information Systems AG) and Daniel Riek (Alcove GmbH).

Friday, October 19th, 2001:

The final day, many more discussions. After a long week everyone heads home; many thanks to Volker Dormeyer who organized and coordinated the booth and watched over it every single day during the last week. Without his help, the FSF Europe could not have presented itself in the way it did!
