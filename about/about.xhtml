About the Free Software Foundation Europe

Vision

The Free Software Foundation Europe (FSF Europe) is a charitable non-governmental organization largely based upon two visions.

Firstly the vision of Free Software as a stable basis for freedom in a digital world -- both in an economic and socio-ethical context. Access to software determines who may participate in a digital society. Therefore the freedoms to use, copy, modify and redistribute software - as described in the Free Software definition - allow equal participation in the information age.

Therefore the FSF Europe is dedicated to all aspects of Free Software in Europe. Creating awareness for these issues, securing Free Software politically and legally, and giving people freedom by supporting development of Free Software are central issues of the FSF Europe, which was founded in 2001 as the European financially, legally and personally independent sister organization of the Free Software Foundation in Boston, MA, USA.

The second vision is that of a Europe united in the spirit of cooperation across cultural boundaries.

Not only do we need to deal with the political reality of the European Union, which already maintains large parts of the governance touching upon the information society, but also we are finding ourselves working in a field determined largely by large multinational companies. These tend to work with a very high level of integration, coordination and consistence between countries on a European and global scale, allowing them to further their interest through "divide and conquer" tactics.

In and with the FSF Europe we stand united and work together on local and international levels, speaking with a voice that cannot be ignored.

Implementation

As an organization based upon the European culture and background, we believe in and are following fundamental principles such as democracy, multilateralism, cooperation, dialog and transparency.

We work hard to live up to these principles as much as possible, but due to limited resources or other problems, we may at times fall short of achieving perfection in them. In these cases, please feel free to help us or get involved either directly or through one of our associate organizations.
