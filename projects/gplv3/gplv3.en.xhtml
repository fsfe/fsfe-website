GPLv3

With this webpage, Free Software Foundation Europe hopes to assist the consultation process for the drafting of version three of the GNU General Public License (GPLv3). Information will be added here as it becomes available, and as we find what questions people have. If you have a suggestion for information which should be here, please contact us.

Introduction: What's happening

On January 16th 2006, Free Software Foundation (FSF), our sister organisation in the United States, published the first discussion draft of the GPL version 3 and launched a 12 month consulation process.

At the end of 2006, FSF will publish the updated the GNU General Public License. This license, commonly called "the GPL", is by far the most used license for Free Software.

The task of the GPL is to ensure that everyone who receives a copy of GPL-licensed software is free to use that software, to modify it, and to redistribute it. The number of people who use software which is distributed under the terms of the GPL is somewhere in the region of 100s of millions.

Version 2 of the GPL was published in 1991. Version 3 will do the same job as version 2 but will be updated to account for changes in software's legal and technical environment.

How to participate

The public consultation is being managed by our sister organisation at the official GPLv3 website. There you can:

  • Sign-up for an account
  • Log-in
  • Submit your comments (after logging in)

By signing-up, you will also be added to an announcement mailing list and will receive infrequent notifications of key GPLv3 events.

Timeline

The following is a timeline for the process with added notes about European GPLv3 events.

2006 January 16th
First public discussion draft is published, along with a rationale document explaining the changes. This marks the beginning of the public consultation process. This occurred during the first international GPLv3 conference.
2006 March 16th
Belfast, Northern Ireland: Richard Stallman will give a talk on topics including GPLv3 at FOSS Means Business.
2006 March 18th
Italy: Richard Stallman will give a presentation on GPLv3 in Torino, Italy, at Italian GPLv3 Conference
2006 May/June
Second draft published after 4 or 5 months of discussion, issue identification, and resolution.
2006 October
Possibly a third draft.
2007 January 15th
Probable release date for the official GPLv3
2007 March
Latest date for release of the official GPLv3

During 2006, as well as the launch conference, there are expected to be at least two other official international GPLv3 conferences as part of the process.
