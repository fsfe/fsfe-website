Free Software PDF Readers

What would you think about a sign on the highway stating “You need a Volkswagen to drive on this road. Contact your Volkswagen dealer for a gratis test drive – Your Government”? When it comes to PDF reading software, many governments do this every day. With the pdfreaders.org campaign we will turn the spotlight on public institutions who behave in this way, exposing how frequently such non-Free advertisements appear.

How to participate

Every day, public institutions advertise non-free software on their websites. With the help of our Fellows we have collected some examples:

Help us to find out where public institutions advertise non-free software. Together we will encourage them to spread Free Software instead.

The pdfreaders.org bug hunt is on from September 13 to October 17, 2010. Join us in hunting down public institutions websites that advertise proprietary PDF readers and report them using the form below.

You can also sign our Petition For The Removal Of Proprietary Software Advertising On Public Websites by ticking the box below.

The three best individual hunters will win a free entry to FSCONS, the world's premier conference for social change in technology taking place in Gothenburg from November 5th to November 7th 2010. Additionally, the winning team and the individual winners will receive a special FSFE gift box.

Report an institution

You can participate in the hunt either as an individual or as a group. Groups will need to register by email to pdfreaders@lists.fsfe.org.

The problem isn't limited to Europe. While we'll limit the follow-up work to European organisations for capacity reasons, reports are welcome from around the world.

The reported bug will first be approved by our team and then be aired, so please be patient before you see your bug report online.
