Our work

Free Software Foundation Europe works to create general understanding and support for software freedom in politics, business, law and society-at-large. We are now involved in many projects and focusing our campaigns on core matters of information society.

  • Freedom Task Force

    The Freedom Task Force is helping individuals, projects and businesses with Free Software licensing. It provides assistance and training. We cooperate with GPL-violations.org and provide documentation including for the GNU GPL.

  • The European Legal Network*

    We maintain the world's largest private, non-partisan, vendor-neutral network of legal experts in the field of Free Software licensing.

  • Open Standards

    Open Standards are key to interoperability, enabling users to escape vendor lock-in. FSFE's goal is making sure that people do not have to lose data when migrating to Free Software.

  • United Nations*

    FSFE is active since 2002 on the UN level, having contributed to making Free Software a world heritage at UNESCO. We are currenlty involved in the following instances:

    • World Intellectual Property Organization
    • Internet Governance Forum

  • European Commission

    FSFE is a third interested party in the European Commission's investigation on Microsoft's monopoly in the domain of Web browsers and the sharing of interoperability information*. We were involved in assisting Samba developers against Microsoft on the agreement on interoperability (see Microsoft versus European Union citizens).

  • Public Awareness

    Working with us are experts who can represent FSFE and serve the Free Software movement at a conference, event or panel discussion.

  • Education

    FSFE actively promote the use of Free Software in schools and universities. Free Software is pedagogically superior, its basic spirit involving freedom and cooperation is the same spirit of education in a democratic environment.

  • Software patents in Europe

    Software patents for Europe are currently being pushed forward actively by a lobby gathering around the European patent office and the Business Software Alliance (BSA), which represents the interests of the large U.S. companies. Software patents are a menace to society and economy.

 

Campaigns

  • PDFreaders.org

    Launched by the Fellowship of FSFE, pdfreaders.org is a site providing information about PDF with links to Free Software PDF readers for all major operating systems.

  • DRM.info

    DRM.info is a collaborative platform initiated and maintained by FSFE to inform on the dangers of Digital Restrictions Management and make visible the concerns from various different groups. DRM.info contributors include digital liberty, consumer protection, net-activism and library organisations.

  • Towards a World Intellectual Wealth Organization

    We endorse and support the Geneva Declaration, and invite its drafters, signatories, and the United Nations to start thinking now not only about what the role of WIPO should be, but rather what kind of organisation we need in its place.

  • "Ask your candidates" - German Federal Election 2009

    During the German federal elections in 2009, FSFE called on all German Free Software supporters to ask the parties' candidates about their positions on Free Software and Open Standards.

The Free Software Foundation Europe has worked on many other issues and campaigns. See our Finished And Discontinued Projects for details of what we've achieved.
